Which US State has Produced the Best NBA Talent?

With the rise of basketball as a talking point in many discussions among friends and family, one question has always been posed: Where do the best NBA Players come from? Through the data set provided by Gabe Salzer at data.world, we can look at the numbers behind which state really boasts the most all-time NBA talent. Looking at the data set, it provides every NBA player since the origin of the league, and their career statistics along with their hometown city and state. From there, you can begin to answer the question of the most NBA talent in a state. Sorting each player by state and counting how many players come from each state, you can eliminate over half of the states in the country. You cannot have the most NBA talent if you do not produce a high quantity of NBA players, even if the players you boast have great numbers. For example, Colorado boasted a couple elite players in Chauncey Billups and Charlie Williams, however in total Colorade has produced just 20 NBA players, far lower than the top states. The states with the highest total number of players are California, New York, and Illinois. California and New York both have over 300, while Illinois has just over 200.

Not only does the state have to produce a lot of NBA players, but the state with the most NBA talent also has to produce good NBA players. I set the benchmark for if an NBA player had a good career with a 15 points per game career average, because if you can average 15 points over the course of your career, statistically you have a high chance of receiving accolades during your career, and should be considered in this elite class of NBA players. After sorting out every player that averaged less than 15 PPG in their career, I tallied the total number of players for each state. New York has 32 players, and California has 31. Illinois, Louisiana and Pennsylvania all also have over 15. Clearly, looking at the data, New York and California are the two states with the greatest NBA talent.

After sifting through the data set, my answer has to be New York. New York has historically had the best basketball players, both in streetball and professionally. There are too many Hall of Fame players born in New York to not say them. Dyckman League Co-Founder Ken Stevens, a legendary New York City streetball league, shares his opinion on the best from the Empire State while acknowledging just how many great players New York has had. “There've been some great guys to come through the city. Some dominated in college like Kenny Anderson and Pearl Washington, a Syracuse guy,” Stevens said. “Dr. J, Chris Mullin, Connie Hawkins, Bernard King, a bunch of guys went on to be perennial all-stars. But you can’t have any other answer for the best hooper from New York than the man Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, formerly known as Lew Alcindor”.

Another reason New York is slightly above California is because their concentration of talent is a little more spread out. There are some great players from Buffalo, Syracuse and Rochester that spread the data out a little more than in just the City.
